## Recovery Tactics: When Posts Flop (NEW)

Not every post will perform. Here's what to do:

### If a Post Gets Low Engagement (< 500 impressions)

**Don't:**
- Delete it (looks bad)
- Post again immediately
- Get discouraged

**Do:**
- Analyze why (weak hook? wrong time? too niche?)
- Engage heavily with others' content (resets algorithm)
- Wait full 24 hours before next post
- Try the same topic with a different hook later

### Common Reasons Posts Flop

| Reason | Fix |
|--------|-----|
| Weak hook | Rewrite with formula from above |
| Too technical | Add relatable story |
| Posted at wrong time | Stick to Tue-Thu 7-8 AM |
| No engagement question | Always end with question |
| Didn't engage before posting | Do the 30-min routine |

### The Recovery Post
After a flop, your next post should be a "safe" format:
- Poll (always gets engagement)
- Story post (3 AM nightmare type)
- Contrarian take (drives comments)

## Analytics Interpretation Guide (NEW)

### Understanding LinkedIn Analytics

**Where to find:** Your profile > Analytics (or Post > View analytics)

### What Each Metric Actually Means

| Metric | What It Tells You | Good Benchmark |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Impressions** | How many feeds your post appeared in | 500+ (new account), 2K+ (established) |
| **Engagement Rate** | (Reactions + Comments + Shares) / Impressions | 3-5% (good), 8%+ (viral) |
| **Click-through Rate** | Profile visits / Impressions | 1-2% |
| **Dwell Time** | How long people spent reading | (Hidden, but affects reach) |

### Reading the Engagement Breakdown


**What matters most:** Comments > Reposts > Reactions > Impressions

### Red Flags in Analytics

| Signal | What It Means | Fix |
|--------|---------------|-----|
| High impressions, low engagement | Hook works, content doesn't deliver | Improve value section |
