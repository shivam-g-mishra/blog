# Amazon Leadership Principles: The Complete Guide

Amazon interviews are defined by Leadership Principles. Every question maps to one or more LPs. Master these, master Amazon interviews.

---

## The 16 Principles

### 1. Customer Obsession

**Leaders start with the customer and work backwards.**

```
Sample Questions:
- "Tell me about a time you went above and beyond for a customer"
- "Describe when you had to make a decision that wasn't best for 
   the customer short-term but was right long-term"

What they want to hear:
✓ Customer-first thinking
✓ Understanding customer needs beyond stated requirements
✓ Measuring success by customer impact
```

### 2. Ownership

**Leaders act on behalf of the entire company. They never say "that's not my job."**

```
Sample Questions:
- "Tell me about a time you took on something outside your area"
- "Describe a project where you had to push through without support"

What they want to hear:
✓ Taking initiative
✓ Long-term thinking
✓ Not blaming others
✓ "I" statements showing personal accountability
```

### 3. Invent and Simplify

**Leaders expect and require innovation and invention.**

```
Sample Questions:
- "Tell me about an innovative solution you created"
- "Describe a time you simplified a complex process"

What they want to hear:
✓ Creative problem-solving
✓ Questioning status quo
✓ Simplifying for customers/team
✓ Measurable improvement
```

### 4. Are Right, A Lot

**Leaders have strong judgment and good instincts.**

```
Sample Questions:
- "Tell me about a time you made a decision with incomplete data"
- "Describe when your judgment differed from others and you were right"

What they want to hear:
✓ Sound decision-making
✓ Seeking diverse perspectives
✓ Being willing to be wrong
✓ Learning from mistakes
```

### 5. Learn and Be Curious

**Leaders are never done learning.**

```
Sample Questions:
- "Tell me about something new you learned recently"
- "Describe how you stay current in your field"

What they want to hear:
✓ Continuous learning
✓ Curiosity beyond your role
✓ Applying new knowledge
✓ Growth mindset
```

### 6. Hire and Develop the Best

**Leaders raise the performance bar with every hire and promotion.**

```
Sample Questions:
- "Tell me about someone you hired or mentored"
- "Describe developing a team member's skills"

What they want to hear:
✓ Mentoring examples
✓ Raising the bar
✓ Recognizing talent
✓ Difficult hiring decisions
```

### 7. Insist on the Highest Standards

**Leaders have relentlessly high standards.**

```
Sample Questions:
- "Tell me about a time you refused to compromise on quality"
- "Describe raising standards on a project"

What they want to hear:
✓ High bar for quality
✓ Not accepting "good enough"
✓ Holding others accountable
✓ Specific quality metrics
```

### 8. Think Big

**Thinking small is a self-fulfilling prophecy.**

```
Sample Questions:
- "Tell me about a bold goal you set"
- "Describe a time you proposed something ambitious"

What they want to hear:
✓ Bold vision
✓ Not limited by constraints
✓ Inspiring others
✓ Long-term impact
```

### 9. Bias for Action

**Speed matters in business. Many decisions are reversible.**

```
Sample Questions:
- "Tell me about a time you had to make a quick decision"
- "Describe acting without waiting for complete information"

What they want to hear:
✓ Taking calculated risks
✓ Moving fast
✓ Not analysis paralysis
✓ Learning from action
```

### 10. Frugality

**Accomplish more with less.**

```
Sample Questions:
- "Tell me about doing more with limited resources"
- "Describe a creative solution that saved money"

What they want to hear:
✓ Resource constraints as innovation drivers
✓ Not just cost-cutting
✓ Creative solutions
✓ Self-sufficiency
```

### 11. Earn Trust

**Leaders listen attentively, speak candidly, and treat others respectfully.**

```
Sample Questions:
- "Tell me about building trust with a skeptical stakeholder"
- "Describe giving difficult feedback"

What they want to hear:
✓ Honest communication
✓ Admitting mistakes
✓ Building relationships
✓ Respectful disagreement
```

### 12. Dive Deep

**Leaders operate at all levels, stay connected to details.**

```
Sample Questions:
- "Tell me about a time you had to dig into details"
- "Describe finding a problem others missed"

What they want to hear:
✓ Getting hands dirty
✓ Data-driven decisions
✓ Attention to detail
✓ Not just delegating
```

### 13. Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit

**Leaders respectfully challenge decisions when they disagree.**

```
Sample Questions:
- "Tell me about disagreeing with your manager"
- "Describe a time you changed your mind"

What they want to hear:
✓ Respectful challenge
✓ Data-backed disagreement
✓ Committing once decided
✓ Not being disagreeable
```

### 14. Deliver Results

**Leaders focus on key inputs and deliver them with the right quality.**

```
Sample Questions:
- "Tell me about a time you delivered under pressure"
- "Describe overcoming obstacles to deliver results"

What they want to hear:
✓ Results orientation
✓ Overcoming obstacles
✓ Never settling
✓ Metrics and outcomes
```

### 15. Strive to be Earth's Best Employer

**Leaders work to create a safer, more productive, higher performing environment.**

```
Sample Questions:
- "Tell me about making work better for your team"
- "Describe helping a struggling colleague"

What they want to hear:
✓ Team development
✓ Work-life balance awareness
✓ Inclusive environment
✓ Employee growth
```

### 16. Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility

**Leaders create more than they consume and leave things better than they found them.**

```
Sample Questions:
- "Tell me about positive impact beyond your immediate work"
- "Describe considering broader implications of a decision"

What they want to hear:
✓ Long-term thinking
✓ Environmental/social awareness
✓ Broader impact consideration
✓ Responsible decision-making
```

## Preparation Strategy

### Build Your Story Matrix

```markdown
| Story | Primary LP | Secondary LPs |
|-------|------------|---------------|
| Project X | Ownership | Deliver Results, Dive Deep |
| Issue Y | Customer Obsession | Bias for Action |
| ... | ... | ... |
```

### Each Story Should Have

- Clear STAR structure
- Quantified results
- Your specific actions ("I" not "we")
- Lessons learned

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Customer Obsession is #1.** Tie everything to customer impact.
2. **Have 2 stories per LP.** Some stories cover multiple.
3. **Use data and metrics.** Amazon is data-driven.
4. **Show ownership.** Never blame others.
5. **Practice out loud.** 2-3 minutes per answer.
