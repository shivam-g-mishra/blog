# Remote Interview Setup Guide

Twenty minutes into my Microsoft interview, my WiFi died.

I was mid-sentence explaining my approach to a graph problem when the video froze. By the time I reconnected (phone hotspot, 3 minutes later), my momentum was gone, my confidence was shaken, and I spent the rest of the interview feeling off-balance.

The frustrating part? This was entirely preventable. I had a backup internet optionâ€”I just hadn't tested it or had it ready to go.

**Remote interviews add a layer of complexity that in-person interviews don't have. Technical issues aren't just annoyingâ€”they can derail your performance.**

Over 70% of coding interviews happen remotely now. This guide ensures technology works for you, not against you.

## Technical Setup Checklist

Complete this checklist **24 hours before** your interview, not 5 minutes before.

### Internet Connection

- [ ] **Test your connection speed** â€” Run speedtest.net. You want at least 10 Mbps upload for stable video.
- [ ] **Use wired ethernet if possible** â€” WiFi is convenient but less reliable
- [ ] **Know your backup plan** â€” Phone hotspot ready? Neighbor's WiFi password?
- [ ] **Close bandwidth-heavy apps** â€” Dropbox syncing, cloud backups, streaming

**If you must use WiFi:**
- Sit closer to your router
- Ask family/roommates to avoid streaming during your interview
- Consider a WiFi extender if your signal is weak

### Audio & Video

- [ ] **Test your microphone** â€” Record yourself speaking. Can you hear clearly?
- [ ] **Test your camera** â€” Is the lighting on your face (not behind you)?
- [ ] **Use headphones with mic** â€” Reduces echo and background noise
- [ ] **Position camera at eye level** â€” Looking down at a laptop camera is unflattering

**Audio quality matters more than video.** If you have to choose, prioritize clear audio over HD video.

### Screen Sharing

- [ ] **Test screen share in advance** â€” Some platforms need permissions first
- [ ] **Close sensitive tabs/apps** â€” Browser bookmarks, Slack, personal email
- [ ] **Clean up your desktop** â€” Remove distracting files and images
- [ ] **Know the keyboard shortcut** â€” Practice starting/stopping share

**Common screen share platforms and their quirks:**

| Platform | Tips |
|----------|------|
| **Zoom** | Use "Share Screen" â†’ specific window (not entire screen) |
| **Google Meet** | Chrome works best; other browsers can have issues |
| **CoderPad/CodeSignal** | Built-inâ€”no separate sharing needed |
| **Microsoft Teams** | May need admin permission on work computers |

### The Coding Environment

- [ ] **Test the interview platform** â€” Log in beforehand if you can
- [ ] **Know how to run code** â€” Where's the "Run" button? How do you see output?
- [ ] **Check language support** â€” Is your preferred language available?
- [ ] **Familiarize with shortcuts** â€” Undo, copy/paste, comment/uncomment

**Practice in the actual platform if possible.** CoderPad, HackerRank, and CodeSignal all have free practice modes.

## Environment Setup

Your physical environment affects both your performance and how you're perceived.

### Lighting

- [ ] **Face a window (natural light) or lamp** â€” Light should be in front of you
- [ ] **Avoid backlighting** â€” Don't sit with a bright window behind you
- [ ] **Check for shadows** â€” Your face should be evenly lit
- [ ] **Test at interview time** â€” Lighting at 2 PM is different from 9 AM

**Quick fix:** A desk lamp positioned behind your laptop, pointing at your face.

### Background

- [ ] **Neutral and uncluttered** â€” Plain wall, bookshelf, or tidy room
- [ ] **Virtual backgrounds OK** â€” If your real background is messy, use one
- [ ] **Remove distractions** â€” No piles of laundry, unmade beds visible
- [ ] **Professional but not sterile** â€” A plant or bookshelf is fine

### Sound Environment

- [ ] **Quiet room with door closed** â€” Tell family/roommates your interview time
- [ ] **Silence phone** â€” Not vibrate. Silent.
- [ ] **Mute desktop notifications** â€” Slack pings are distracting
- [ ] **Put pets in another room** â€” Dogs barking is endearing once, annoying twice

**Reasonable interruptions won't hurt you.** If a delivery person rings the doorbell once or your cat walks across your keyboard, the interviewer will understand. Just handle it briefly and professionally: "Sorry about thatâ€”let me continue where I left off."

</ConfidenceBuilder>

## Backup Plan

Things will go wrong. Have a plan for when they do.

### Must-Have Ready

| Backup | Why | How to Prepare |
|--------|-----|----------------|
| **Phone hotspot** | Internet backup | Test it works, know how to enable quickly |
| **Interviewer's email** | Send code if screen share fails | Copy before interview |
| **Phone number** | Audio backup | Have it visible during interview |
| **Alternative device** | Computer crashes | Tablet or phone, logged into platform |

### The Recovery Message

If tech fails, immediately send (via platform chat, email, or text):

> "Technical difficulties on my endâ€”working on reconnecting. Back in 2 minutes."

Then reconnect. Don't spend 10 minutes troubleshooting. Switch to your backup.

**Interviewers generally give 5+ minutes grace for tech issues.** But after that, time does count against your interview.

## Interview Day Protocol

### 30 Minutes Before

- [ ] Close unnecessary applications
- [ ] Open the interview link (but don't join yet)
- [ ] Open a backup text editor
- [ ] Have water within reach
- [ ] Use the bathroom

### 10 Minutes Before

- [ ] Join the waiting room if available
- [ ] Do a final audio/video check
- [ ] Take 5 deep breaths
- [ ] Review your "I am prepared" notes

### 5 Minutes Before

- [ ] Ready to join
- [ ] Posture checkâ€”sit up straight
- [ ] Smileâ€”it changes your voice tone
- [ ] Interviewer info visible (their name, role)

## Platform-Specific Tips

### CoderPad (Common for FAANG)

- **Practice mode available** at coderpad.io/sandbox
- Default language is usually changeable
- Output appears in the console below
- Drawing mode available for diagrams
- Interviewer can see your cursor and edits in real-time

### HackerRank (Common for initial screens)

- Often auto-gradedâ€”test cases matter
- "Run Code" vs "Submit"â€”know the difference
- Can't see test case inputs (usually)
- Time limits are enforced

### Google Docs (Still used by some)

- No syntax highlighting
- No running code
- Focus on clarity and communication
- Use consistent indentation

### Excalidraw/Miro (For system design)

- Practice drawing boxes and arrows
- Learn keyboard shortcuts for shapes
- Keep diagrams cleanâ€”don't overcrowd

## Quick Reference Card

### Before Interview
| Task | When | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| Test internet speed | 24 hours before | Identify issues early |
| Check camera/mic | 24 hours before | Get better equipment if needed |
| Practice in platform | 24 hours before | No surprises |
| Close apps, clean desktop | 30 min before | Less distraction |
| Join waiting room | 5-10 min before | Confirm everything works |

### If Things Go Wrong
| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| **Video freezes** | Turn off video, continue with audio |
| **Audio fails** | Switch to phone call immediately |
| **Internet drops** | Switch to phone hotspot |
| **Screen share fails** | Describe your code verbally, email if needed |
| **Platform crashes** | Message interviewer, switch to backup |
