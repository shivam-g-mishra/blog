# Apple Interview Guide

Apple interviews are unique: high secrecy, team-specific process, and emphasis on passion for the product.

---

## Interview Process

```
1. Recruiter Screen (30 min)
   └── Background, interest in Apple

2. Phone Screen (45-60 min)
   └── Technical coding or domain-specific

3. Onsite / Virtual Loop (5-8 rounds)
   ├── Coding × 2-3
   ├── System Design × 1-2 (Senior+)
   ├── Domain expertise × 1-2
   └── Behavioral × 1-2 (hiring manager)

4. Executive Review
   └── Senior leadership approval
```

---

## Apple Culture

| Value | What They Look For |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Secrecy** | Can you keep confidential work private? |
| **Product passion** | Do you love Apple products? |
| **Attention to detail** | Pixel-perfect, no shortcuts |
| **Collaboration** | Cross-functional teamwork |
| **Excellence** | Best-in-class, not just good enough |

---

## Unique Aspects

### Product Passion

```
Be ready to answer:
- "Why Apple?"
- "What's your favorite Apple product and why?"
- "What would you improve about [product]?"
- "How do you use Apple products in your life?"

Tips:
- Be genuine, not generic
- Show you've thought deeply about their products
- Mention specific features, not just "it's beautiful"
```

### Secrecy Culture

```
Expect questions about:
- Working on secret projects
- Not discussing work with friends/family
- Compartmentalized teams

Show you can:
- Handle confidential information
- Work without public recognition
- Respect need-to-know boundaries
```

## Technical Expectations

### Coding

- Strong fundamentals (data structures, algorithms)
- Clean, production-quality code
- Domain-relevant skills (iOS, macOS, hardware, etc.)

### System Design (Senior+)

- Scalability considerations
- Privacy and security focus
- Integration with Apple ecosystem
- Hardware-software optimization

---

## Team-Specific Focus

### iOS/macOS Engineering
- Swift, Objective-C
- UIKit, SwiftUI
- Memory management
- Performance optimization

### Services (iCloud, Apple Music, etc.)
- Distributed systems
- Scalability
- Privacy-preserving architecture

### Hardware
- Systems programming
- Low-level optimization
- Embedded systems

### Machine Learning
- On-device ML (Core ML)
- Privacy-preserving techniques
- Performance constraints

---

## Sample Questions

### Behavioral

- "Why Apple over Google/Meta?"
- "Tell me about a time you obsessed over details"
- "How do you handle working on something you can't talk about?"

### Technical

- Standard coding problems
- Domain-specific questions
- "How would you design [Apple feature]?"

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Show genuine product passion.** Know Apple products deeply.
2. **Attention to detail matters.** Don't cut corners.
3. **Be comfortable with secrecy.** It's core to Apple culture.
4. **Process is team-specific.** Research your target team.
5. **Excellence over speed.** Quality is paramount.
